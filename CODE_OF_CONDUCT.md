# Code of Conduct

SafeExtensions is a privacy-first, security-focused project. We aim to provide a welcoming, professional space for everyone.

## Scope
This Code applies to all project spaces and interactions: issues, pull requests, discussions, docs, code reviews, and community channels. It applies to maintainers, contributors, and users.

## Standards
We expect all participants to:
- Be respectful, considerate, and constructive.
- Assume good intent and seek clarity before escalating.
- Focus on technical merits and documented evidence.
- Protect user privacy and follow security best practices.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, doxxing, threats, or sustained disruption. Sexualized language or imagery and unrelated political or ideological advocacy are out of scope here.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness a problem:
- Prefer direct maintainer reporting for sensitive matters via GitHub: https://github.com/CSroseX/SafeExtensions/issues
- For non-sensitive issues, you may open a discussion or issue using a neutral title (for example, “Code of Conduct Concern”).
- If you believe there is immediate risk, stop engagement and notify the maintainers.

Reports should include: what happened, where, links/screenshots if available, and any context you think helps. We will respect requests for confidentiality to the extent possible.

## Enforcement
Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warnings, edit/lock of threads, temporary or permanent bans, and escalation to platform support when necessary. Repeated or severe violations may lead to permanent removal from the community spaces.

## Attribution
This policy is informed by widely used community guidelines (including the Contributor Covenant) but simplified for clarity and fit to SafeExtensions.
